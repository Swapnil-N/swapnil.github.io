## Denver

Flew into Denver and spent the first evening exploring the city. Grabbed dinner in RiNo, checked out a couple of breweries (as is tradition in Denver), and enjoyed the views of the Front Range as the sun set. Denver is always a solid launching pad for a mountain adventure.

## Steamboat Springs

The drive up to Steamboat was gorgeous — snow-covered peaks and winding mountain roads. Steamboat lives up to the hype. The champagne powder is real — light, dry, and absolutely dreamy to carve through. We spent three full days on the mountain, hitting everything from groomed cruisers to some gnarly tree runs in the back bowls.

After skiing, the town itself is a great hang. We soaked in the natural hot springs, which is the perfect way to recover after a day on the slopes. The food scene was surprisingly good for a small mountain town, and the apres-ski vibes were exactly what you'd hope for. Already planning the next trip back.
