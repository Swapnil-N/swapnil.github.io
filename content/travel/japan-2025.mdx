## Tokyo

Tokyo is sensory overload in the most exhilarating way. We timed the trip for cherry blossom season and the city delivered — parks blanketed in pink, hanami picnics under the trees, petals drifting through the air like confetti. Shibuya Crossing, Shinjuku at night, the quiet intensity of Meiji Shrine, and the absolute madness of Akihabara — Tokyo packs a hundred different experiences into every day. The food alone could justify the trip: ramen shops, sushi at Tsukiji Outer Market, and convenience store onigiri that somehow rivals restaurant food.

## Kyoto

Kyoto was the perfect counterbalance to Tokyo's intensity. The temples and shrines here are otherworldly — Fushimi Inari's thousand torii gates, the golden pavilion of Kinkaku-ji, and the bamboo groves of Arashiyama. We rented bikes and spent a day just riding through the old streets, stumbling onto hidden gardens and tiny tea houses. The cherry blossoms along the Philosopher's Path were peak perfection. Kyoto moves at a different pace, and it felt good to slow down.

## Osaka

Osaka is where the food gets serious. Dotonbori at night is a neon-lit food paradise — takoyaki, okonomiyaki, and kushikatsu everywhere you turn. We ate our way through the city with zero regrets. Osaka Castle was impressive, and the city has a grittier, more laid-back energy compared to Tokyo. The people are famously friendly and funny, and you can feel it everywhere. A perfect way to close out the Japan trip.
