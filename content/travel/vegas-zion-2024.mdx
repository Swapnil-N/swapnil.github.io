## Las Vegas

Started the trip with a couple nights in Vegas. The strip at night is still an absolute sensory overload in the best way. Caught a show, ate at a few spots that were way too expensive but totally worth it, and managed to walk away from the blackjack table only slightly lighter in the wallet. Vegas is always a good time when you don't take it too seriously.

## Zion National Park

The drive from Vegas to Zion is stunning — the landscape just transforms as you cross into Utah. Zion blew my mind. The sheer scale of the canyon walls towering above you is hard to process. Did Angels Landing, which was every bit as intense as people say — narrow ridgeline, chain handholds, thousand-foot drops on either side. The views from the top made every white-knuckle moment worth it. The Narrows was a completely different vibe — wading through the Virgin River with canyon walls closing in around you.

## Grand Canyon

From Zion, we drove down to the South Rim of the Grand Canyon. No matter how many photos you've seen, nothing prepares you for standing on the edge and looking out across that vast, layered abyss. We hiked partway down Bright Angel Trail, watched the sunset paint the canyon walls in shades of orange and red, and just sat in awe. It's the kind of place that makes you feel very, very small — in a good way.
