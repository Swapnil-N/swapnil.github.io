## Mexico City

CDMX is a megacity that somehow still feels intimate. Every neighborhood — or colonia — has its own distinct personality, and we spent the week hopping between them. Roma and Condesa are the trendy spots with tree-lined streets, art deco buildings, and incredible restaurants on every block. Coyoacan feels like a small town with the Frida Kahlo Museum as its beating heart. Centro Historico is grand, chaotic, and layered with centuries of history.

The food is the star of the show, without question. Tacos al pastor from street stands that have been perfecting the craft for decades. Mole that took three days to make. Churros and hot chocolate at El Moro. Fresh-squeezed juices on every corner. CDMX might be the best food city in the world, and I will argue that point passionately.

We visited Teotihuacan and climbed the Pyramid of the Sun, which gives you a perspective on just how advanced this civilization was. The Anthropology Museum downtown is one of the best museums I've ever been to — you could spend days in there. Mexico City is massive, vibrant, delicious, and endlessly surprising. A week was not enough.
