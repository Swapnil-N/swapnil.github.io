## Santiago & Volcan Santiago

Guatemala's highlands are something else entirely. The air is thinner, the colors are more vivid, and everywhere you look there's a volcano on the horizon. We based ourselves near Santiago and spent the trip exploring the surrounding area and preparing for the main event — the hike up Volcan Santiago.

The hike itself was challenging and absolutely rewarding. The trail winds through cloud forest before breaking out above the treeline, where the volcanic landscape takes over. The terrain is loose rock and ash, and the altitude makes every step feel like two. But reaching the summit and looking down into the crater, with plumes of sulfurous steam rising from the vents, was one of the most raw and powerful things I've ever seen.

The towns around the volcano were beautiful in their own right — colorful markets, incredible local food, and some of the friendliest people I've met anywhere. Guatemala doesn't get enough credit as a travel destination, and I'd go back in a heartbeat.
