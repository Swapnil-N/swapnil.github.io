## India

Three weeks in India is both a lifetime and not nearly enough. This trip was about connection — reconnecting with family, with roots, and with a country that runs on a completely different frequency. India doesn't ease you in; it hits you with everything at once — the colors, the sounds, the smells, the heat, the crowds, the warmth of the people. It's overwhelming and beautiful in equal measure.

The food alone made the trip worthwhile ten times over. Home-cooked meals with family that tasted like memory itself. Street food that ranged from transcendent to adventurous. Chai on every corner, served in tiny clay cups that you toss when you're done. Every region has its own cuisine, and every meal told a story.

Visiting temples, markets, and historic sites gave the trip a deeper layer. There's a spirituality woven into everyday life here that's hard to find anywhere else. Early mornings at the ghats, evenings watching the sunset over ancient architecture, and long conversations with family that stretched late into the night.

This wasn't just a vacation — it was a pilgrimage of sorts. India has a way of shaking you up and putting you back together, and I left feeling more connected to where I come from than ever before.
