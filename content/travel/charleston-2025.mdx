## Charleston

Charleston is one of those cities that feels like stepping into a postcard — pastel-colored houses, cobblestone streets, Spanish moss dripping from ancient oaks, and a waterfront that catches the most incredible sunsets. The history here is layered and complex, and we spent time exploring both the beautiful architecture and the deeper stories behind the city.

The food, though — that's what really makes Charleston shine. Lowcountry cuisine is comfort food elevated to an art form. Shrimp and grits that made me reconsider every brunch I've ever had, she-crab soup, fresh oysters by the waterfront, and some of the best fried chicken south of the Mason-Dixon line. The restaurant scene here punches way above its weight for a city this size.

We walked the Battery, explored Rainbow Row, toured a couple of the historic plantations, and spent an evening just wandering King Street. July in Charleston means serious heat and humidity, but the sweet tea stays cold and the ocean breeze helps. It's a charming, complicated, beautiful city, and I'd love to come back in the fall.
