## Rio de Janeiro

Rio during Carnival is unlike anything else on the planet. The entire city becomes one massive party — samba schools parading through the Sambadrome, blocos (street parties) taking over entire neighborhoods, and music pounding from every direction at all hours. It's joyful, chaotic, beautiful, and completely overwhelming in the best possible way.

We spent our days bouncing between blocos in Santa Teresa and Lapa, dancing with strangers, and trying to keep up with the energy of a city that simply does not sleep during Carnival. The Sambadrome parade at night was the highlight — the floats, the costumes, the drummers, the dancers. The scale and artistry of it all is staggering.

Beyond Carnival, Rio itself is stunning. Christ the Redeemer watching over the city from Corcovado, the iconic curve of Copacabana Beach, the views from Sugarloaf Mountain at sunset — it's a city that looks like it was designed to be beautiful. The food was incredible too — acai bowls on the beach, churrasco, and fresh tropical juices everywhere. Rio left me exhausted and completely alive.
