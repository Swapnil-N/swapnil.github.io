## Detroit

There's something surreal about standing in a city known for Motown and muscle cars, watching the moon slowly swallow the sun. I flew into Detroit a few days early to explore the city before the main event, and I'm glad I did. The downtown revival is real — great restaurants, cool murals, and the Detroit Institute of Arts blew me away.

The day of the eclipse, the energy was electric. Crowds gathered along the riverfront, everyone with their glasses tilted skyward. When totality hit, the temperature dropped, the streetlights flickered on, and for about three and a half minutes, the world went still. The corona glowing around the black disc of the moon is something no photo can capture — you just have to be there.

After the eclipse, I spent a couple more days exploring. Hit up the Henry Ford Museum, drove through some of the neighborhoods seeing the comeback story firsthand, and ate my weight in Detroit-style pizza. The whole trip felt like a perfect blend of cosmic wonder and gritty Americana.
