## Munich

Munich during Oktoberfest is pure organized chaos — and I mean that as the highest compliment. The beer tents are massive, the energy is incredible, and there's something deeply unifying about sitting at a long table with strangers from every corner of the world, singing songs you don't know the words to while hoisting a one-liter stein. We got into a couple of the big tents and the atmosphere was electric.

Beyond the festival, Munich is a beautiful city. The Marienplatz, the English Garden, and the views of the Alps on a clear day are all stunning. The food is hearty and perfect for the occasion — pretzels the size of your head, schnitzel, and weisswurst for breakfast. Munich knows how to do tradition right.

## Berlin

From Munich, we took the train up to Berlin, and the contrast is striking. Berlin is edgy, creative, and still processing its heavy history in fascinating ways. The East Side Gallery, the Holocaust Memorial, Checkpoint Charlie, and the remnants of the Wall — it's a city that doesn't shy away from its past. The nightlife is legendary, and we experienced a taste of it in Kreuzberg and Friedrichshain. The street art, the food markets, and the general spirit of the city make Berlin one of the most interesting places in Europe.
