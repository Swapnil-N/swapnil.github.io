## San Francisco

San Francisco in December has a moody, romantic quality to it. The fog rolls in over the Golden Gate, the city lights reflect off the bay, and there's a crispness to the air that makes every walk feel cinematic. We spent the week exploring neighborhoods — the Mission for burritos and murals, North Beach for Italian food and City Lights Bookstore, the Haight for vintage shops and counterculture vibes.

The food scene in SF is world-class and we took full advantage. Dim sum in Chinatown, sourdough at Boudin, incredible seafood at the Embarcadero, and some of the best farm-to-table dining in the country. The city's relationship with food is serious and it shows in every meal.

We biked across the Golden Gate Bridge on a rare clear day, explored Alcatraz (book way in advance), and took in sunset views from Dolores Park. The hills are no joke — my calves were wrecked by day three — but every climb rewards you with a view that makes it worth it. SF has a magic to it that's hard to explain until you've been there.
